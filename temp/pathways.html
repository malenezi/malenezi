Learning Pathways

Stackable, role-based journeys from first principles to mastery — with a diagnostic start, milestone stages, and multiple ways in and out.

How a pathway works

A journey, not a course list.

A pathway is an ordered route through the framework toward a role. It begins with a diagnostic that places the learner at the right level, moves through milestone stages that each build a coherent body of competence, and offers named exit points where the learner is already employable.

Pathways are stackable: every stage completed earns a credential that counts toward the next. And lateral bridges connect pathways, so a Data Analyst can cross into Engineering or Science without starting over.

Pathway design
  • Diagnostic-led — no learner starts in the wrong place
  • Milestone stages, each a coherent competence set
  • Multiple named, employable exit points
  • Stackable — every stage earns a credential
  • Lateral bridges between related pathways

The journey

Six stages, from diagnose to lead.

Every pathway follows the same six-stage shape. This is the AI Engineer journey — the worked example for the framework.

Stage 0 · Diagnose

Diagnostic placement

A short diagnostic assesses existing competence and places the learner at the right entry point — no time wasted on what is already known.

Stage 1 · Foundations

Build the base  ·  Level 1

Core programming, data handling, and machine-learning fundamentals — the shared foundation every AI Engineer needs.

Exit point: Foundations micro-credential
Stage 2 · Core Engineer

Core craft  ·  Level 2

Model development, deployment, and MLOps — plus the responsible-AI gate that must be cleared before any specialisation.

Gate: Responsible-AI review · Exit: Professional Certificate
Stage 3 · Specialise

Choose a sub-track  ·  Level 3

One of five sub-tracks — AI Applications, MLOps, Fine-tuning, Agents, or Infrastructure — for production-grade depth.

Exit point: Advanced Diploma
Stage 4 · Lead & Operate

Lead the work  ·  Level 4

Technical leadership — architecture, multi-team delivery, and advising the organisation on AI strategy.

Stage 5 · Strategic

Set direction  ·  Level 5

Enterprise AI vision, investment, and national alignment — the chief-level exit point of the pathway.

Exit point: Executive Credential
Specialisation

Five sub-tracks at Stage 3.

Once the core craft and responsible-AI gate are complete, the AI Engineer pathway branches into five specialisation sub-tracks.

Sub-track A

AI Applications

Building user-facing AI products and features — from RAG systems to production copilots.

Sub-track B

MLOps

The discipline of running models in production — pipelines, automation, and reliability.

Sub-track C

Fine-tuning

Adapting foundation models to Saudi-specific tasks, languages, and domains.

Sub-track D

AI Agents

Designing autonomous and tool-using agentic systems and the guardrails they require.

Sub-track E

AI Infrastructure

The compute and platform layer — sovereign, scalable infrastructure for national AI.

Cross-cutting

Lateral bridges

Bridge modules let learners cross between pathways — Analyst to Engineer, Engineer to Science.
