The Competency Model

What capability actually means — a three-tier model of measurable, observable, and assessable competencies for every Data & AI role.

The model

Three tiers of capability.

Each occupation's competencies are organised into three tiers. Technical skills define what a professional can build; responsible-AI practices define how they must build it; and professional enablers define how they work within an organisation.

The model shown here is the worked example for the AI Engineer occupation — eighteen competencies in total. The same three-tier structure applies to every role in the framework.

The three tiers

Build it, build it responsibly, work well.

Each tier has its own refresh cadence — the faster a tier's knowledge ages, the more often it is reviewed.

Tier 01

Technical Skills

The engineering craft — framing problems, building data pipelines, developing and deploying models, and operating them reliably in production.

8 competencies · refreshed every 6 months
Tier 02

Responsible AI

The non-negotiable practice — ethics, fairness, privacy, safety, and accountability — gated before any learner can specialise.

5 competencies · refreshed every 12 months
Tier 03

Professional Enablers

The durable human capabilities — communication, collaboration, strategic thinking, and accountable judgement.

5 competencies · refreshed every 24 months

Proficiency scale

Five levels, one measurable scale.

Every competency is assessed against the same five-level proficiency scale, each level defined by observable behaviour.

L · 01

Aware

Foundational

Understands core concepts and can describe the competency in context.

L · 02

Applied

Working

Performs the competency on routine tasks with guidance and review.

L · 03

Proficient

Independent

Performs reliably and unsupervised on production-grade work.

L · 04

Advanced

Leading

Handles complex, ambiguous problems and guides others' practice.

L · 05

Expert

Authority

Sets direction, innovates, and is a recognised reference in the field.

How it is measured

Assessment that proves capability.

Competency is evidenced through authentic assessment — not recall. Four assessment modes are used across the framework.

The primary mode of assessment. Learners produce real, production-grade artefacts — a deployed model, a data pipeline, an evaluation report — assessed against a four-band rubric mapped directly to competency proficiency levels.

Observed performance of a skill in a lab or sandbox environment, used where a competency is best evidenced by watching it done — debugging a failing pipeline, calibrating a model, responding to drift.

Targeted assessment of conceptual knowledge that underpins practice, used to confirm the knowledge (K) elements of a job card before a learner advances to applied work.

A dedicated checkpoint before specialisation. Learners must evidence the Tier-2 responsible-AI competencies through a review of how they identified and mitigated ethical, fairness, and privacy risk in their project work.
