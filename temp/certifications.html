Stackable Certifications

A four-tier credentialing framework on the Open Badges 3.0 standard — every credential portable, verifiable, and traceable to the national standard.

The principle

Learning that compounds.

In the framework, learning is never lost. Every credential is valuable on its own and counts toward a larger one — a micro-badge stacks into a professional certificate, which stacks into an advanced diploma, which stacks toward an executive credential.

This is what makes the framework navigable. A learner can stop at any tier with a recognised, employable credential — and resume later exactly where they left off.

The four tiers

From micro-badge to executive credential.

Four tiers of credential, each a stacking step toward the next — and each meaningful in the labour market on its own.

Tier 01

Micro-badge

Evidences a single competency at a defined proficiency level. Earned on completion of a module with authentic assessment. The atomic unit of the credential system.

Earned per module · stacks into a certificate
Tier 02

Professional Certificate

Evidences a coherent cluster of competencies — typically a pathway stage. The first credential that signals job-readiness for a role at entry level.

Earned per pathway stage · employable exit point
Tier 03

Advanced Diploma

Evidences specialised, production-grade depth in a sub-track. Recognises a professional who can work independently at the principal level of the career ladder.

Earned per specialisation · principal-level signal
Tier 04

Executive Credential

Evidences strategic and leadership capability at the expert and chief levels — the apex credential of a pathway.

Earned at L4–L5 · chief-level signal
Open Badges 3.0

Verifiable by design.

Every credential is issued on Open Badges 3.0 — the 1EdTech standard built on W3C Verifiable Credentials.

Property 01

Cryptographically verifiable

Each credential can be verified for authenticity without contacting the issuer — tamper-evident by design.

Property 02

Competency-aligned

Every badge carries an alignment block naming the exact NOSF competency codes and proficiency levels it evidences.

Property 03

Learner-owned & portable

Credentials live in the learner's own digital wallet — portable across employers, platforms, and borders.

Property 04

Interoperable

Built on open W3C and 1EdTech standards — no vendor lock-in, and recognised by any conformant system.

Recognition

Four routes to recognition.

A credential is only as useful as the doors it opens. The framework builds recognition through four channels.

Because every credential names the precise competencies it evidences, employers can map credentials directly onto job requirements — making them usable in hiring, placement, and promotion decisions across government and the private sector.

The framework is designed so credentials can articulate toward formal qualifications — recognised prior learning that gives credit toward university programs and reduces duplication of study.

Credentials are designed to align with the national qualifications structure, so a learner's progress through the framework is recognised within the wider Saudi education and training system.

Built on open international standards and benchmarked against global frameworks, the credentials are calibrated to be understood and trusted beyond the kingdom — supporting an internationally mobile workforce.
